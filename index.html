Research Opportunity

Electronic Waste as a Multidisciplinary Research Opportunity

Future electronic waste systems raise interconnected research questions across materials recovery, environmental engineering, automation, sensing, AI-assisted workflows, lifecycle assessment, data governance, and compliance-aware evidence systems.

We are using Ex-Spec as a conceptual basis for discussing how these domains may intersect in future responsible recovery infrastructure. This framing does not claim that Wrek’d Tech currently solves these problems, operates a recovery system, or provides ITAD services.

Ex-Spec Concept

Ex-Spec: A Conceptual System for Advanced E-Waste Recovery Research

Ex-Spec is presented as a conceptual system for discussing how future e-waste recovery could integrate input stream review, identification and sensing, process intelligence, robotics and automation, materials recovery, compliance-aware evidence, and environmental or lifecycle review.

  • Input stream review
  • Identification / sensing
  • Process intelligence
  • Robotics and automation
  • Materials recovery
  • Compliance-aware evidence
  • Environmental / lifecycle review

Ex-Spec is conceptual. It is not a physical prototype, validated technology, operating facility, commercial recycling system, deployed process, or available ITAD service.

Collaboration Model

A Low-Risk Framework for Exploratory Academic Engagement

The model below is non-binding and designed to clarify fit before any formal activity is considered. It does not imply automatic progression, existing partnership, endorsement, funding, or approval.

  1. Introductory Academic Conversation

    Clarify research interests, institutional fit, and appropriate boundaries.

  2. R&D Fit Review

    Identify whether Ex-Spec-related questions align with faculty expertise, labs, programs, or applied research formats.

  3. Concept Evaluation Discussion

    Discuss technical unknowns, feasibility questions, evaluation methods, and responsible limits.

  4. Student or Applied Research Exploration

    Consider whether a future capstone, practicum, studio, or applied research format could be appropriate.

  5. Formal Documentation if Appropriate

    Move to written documentation only if both parties determine there is a defined next step.

Any collaboration, research activity, student project, pilot, grant participation, advisory role, or institutional engagement would require separate written review and agreement by the appropriate parties.
