Flagship Concept

Ex-Spec: A Conceptual Advanced E-Waste Recovery System

Ex-Spec is our conceptual framework for exploring advanced approaches to electronic waste recovery, material identification, process intelligence, and responsible circular economy infrastructure.

The concept is intended to support academic and technical discussion around how future e-waste systems could integrate materials science, environmental engineering, sensing technologies, robotics, automation, artificial intelligence, compliance-aware workflows, and lifecycle analysis.

We are presenting Ex-Spec as a research subject for multidisciplinary inquiry. It is not currently a physical prototype, validated technology, operating facility, commercial recycling system, or available ITAD service.

Ex-Spec remains conceptual. It has not been validated, deployed, commercialized, or operated as a processing system.

Collaboration Pathways

A Non-Binding Framework for Exploratory Academic Engagement

We welcome structured preliminary conversations with academic and applied innovation stakeholders to determine whether Ex-Spec-related questions may align with faculty expertise, laboratory capabilities, student learning objectives, or institutional research priorities.

The purpose of these conversations is not to presume partnership, endorsement, or project approval. The purpose is to determine whether there is a responsible basis for continued discussion, technical review, or future documentation.

Any collaboration, research activity, student project, pilot, grant participation, advisory role, or institutional engagement would require separate written review and agreement by the appropriate parties.

Non-Binding Pathway Stages

Introductory Academic Conversation

Clarify interests, research context, institutional fit, and appropriate boundaries for further discussion.

Faculty or Lab Fit Review

Compare Ex-Spec-related questions with relevant faculty expertise, departmental priorities, or laboratory capabilities.

Concept Feasibility Discussion

Identify technical unknowns, research constraints, validation needs, and responsible evaluation methods.

Student Project Exploration

Consider whether a future capstone, practicum, studio, or applied research format could be appropriate.

Future Proof-of-Concept Planning

Discuss what evidence, resources, documentation, and institutional approvals would be required before any future proof-of-concept activity.

Formal Documentation Later

Use written agreements only if both parties determine that a defined next step is appropriate.
